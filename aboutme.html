Biography

I am Korean-American, and often, because of my ethinicity, racial prejudices create disadvantages for me. As an Asian woman, many assume that I am passive and submissive, and because of that, I am constantly trying to prove myself. This time spent trying to convince others that I am a candidate worthy of being recognized results in time wasted. Instead I could be making improvements and advancements to human rights and reshaping the way women are viewed.

I enjoy eating new things. In fact, I have even tried escargot! I am ambitious in the things I want to achieve and I am always looking forward to the future. I am confident in the advancement of women's rights because I, along with my peers, are striving to break those social barriers. In the history of the United States, with every advancement for the country, women have spoken out, demanding for representation alongside the men of the nation. The taking down of the traditional domestic sphere was one of the first large advancements, and resulted in more progress regarding women's rights.

I hope that I can be a pioneer for women's rights and a role model for girls all over the world.
